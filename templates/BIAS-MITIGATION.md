# Layer 3: Bias Mitigation — 14 Questions
## 7 Cognitive Distortions → 2 Questions Each

> Run after all hardware/soft gates have cleared and the remaining residual
> doubt is emotional, not structural.
>
> Scoring: Each answer is RESOLVED (clean, decisive, no hedging) or
> UNRESOLVED (rationalization, deflection, self-contradiction, deferred).
>
> Any UNRESOLVED answer → loop reopens. Do not skip to verdict.

---

### 1. Confirmation Bias
*"You seek information supporting your choice while ignoring contradictory evidence."*

**Q1.1:** What's the strongest argument AGAINST this decision that you haven't already factored into the analysis? Be specific — not "it's expensive" but a concrete, unique negative.

**Q1.2:** If a friend came to you with this exact same situation, what would you advise? Would your advice match what you're about to do?

---

### 2. Overconfidence
*"You trust your judgment more than it deserves, assuming your logic is flawless."*

**Q2.1:** What would have to be true for this to be a mistake 12 months from now? Three specific failure scenarios, not vague.

**Q2.2:** What if your interests shift in 6 months toward a direction where this machine underperforms? How likely, and what's the fallback?

---

### 3. Anxiety / Stress
*"Emotional pressure clouds judgment, making you second-guess even sound decisions."*

**Q3.1:** What part of this decision feels most emotionally heavy right now? Name it precisely — not "I'm stressed" but "I'm afraid of X."

**Q3.2:** If you removed all emotion, what's the minimum utilization to break even? Give a number (hours/week, projects/month, cost avoided).

---

### 4. Loss Aversion
*"Fear of failure distorts risk assessment, leading to hesitation."*

**Q4.1:** You're not actually afraid of losing the money. What are you really afraid of losing? Reputation? Trust? Sense of discipline? The alternative (not having the capability)?

**Q4.2:** Which regret would hurt more in 3 years — buying and underutilizing, or NOT buying and failing at a project for lack of hardware?

---

### 5. Social Pressure
*"External expectations undermine confidence in your choice."*

**Q5.1:** Who in your life would think this is reckless? Whose opinion is shaping the guilt more than your own analysis?

**Q5.2:** If no one else ever knew you bought this, would you still buy it? If the answer changes, the purchase partly signals — and it's too expensive for signaling.

---

### 6. Complexity Paralysis
*"Too many variables make it hard to pinpoint certainty, creating doubt."*

**Q6.1:** If you could keep only ONE piece of information from all analysis to make the call, what is it?

**Q6.2:** If the machine would definitely break in exactly 14 months, at what price would you still buy it? That's your true value threshold. How does the actual price compare?

---

### 7. Biological Variation
*"Real-world decisions aren't static — natural fluctuations introduce irreducible uncertainty."*

**Q7.1:** Name the three biggest irreducible uncertainties (e.g., battery health, resale price, warranty response time). Can you live with each at their worst realistic case?

**Q7.2:** What's the realistic worst-case outcome, written in one sentence without cascade? Is it survivable?
