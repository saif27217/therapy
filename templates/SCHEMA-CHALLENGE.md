# Layer 4: Schema Challenge — 10 Questions
## 5 Maladaptive Belief Patterns → 2 Questions Each

> Run after bias mitigation. This layer probes deeper: maladaptive core beliefs
> and cognitive schemas that override rational verdicts even when bias check passes.
>
> These are NOT about the decision itself. They are about the DECIDER's pattern
> of responding to fear, doubt, and self-trust.
>
> Scoring: Same as Layer 3 — RESOLVED or UNRESOLVED.
> Any UNRESOLVED → loop reopens with targeted reframing exercise.

---

### 1. Emotional Override
*"A deeply held core belief triggers anxiety/fear, making you second-guess even when facts support the decision."*

**Q1:** What is the specific feeling that rises when you imagine completing this decision? Name it in one word. Is this feeling protecting you from real harm, or from a *belief* about harm?

**Q2:** If this feeling were a person whispering in your ear, what would it say verbatim? Has this exact sentence stopped you from doing something beneficial before?

---

### 2. Cognitive Dissonance
*"When action conflicts with core belief, the mind creates discomfort and may dismiss evidence to resolve it."*

**Q3:** What core belief about yourself does this decision *conflict* with? Examples: "I'm responsible with money," "I only spend on necessities," "I'm not the kind of person who invests in themselves."

**Q4:** The analysis supports the decision, yet doubt persists. What part of you *wants* this to be wrong? What would you gain from proving it's a bad idea?

---

### 3. Overgeneralization
*"Maladaptive schemas amplify worst-case scenarios. Logic says 'sound' but schema says 'everything will go wrong.'"*

**Q5:** When you imagine this going wrong, do you see *this specific failure* or do you see *your whole life cascading* — family disappointment, financial ruin, career collapse? If cascade, that's overgeneralization.

**Q6:** What's the *smallest* realistic negative outcome? Not the worst case — the *minimum* harm. Is that minimum survivable? If yes, threat assessment is inflated.

---

### 4. Schema Rigidity (Lack of Permeability)
*"Rigid schemas resist new evidence. The brain clings to old patterns because they feel familiar, even when harmful."*

**Q7:** Is there a time you made a well-researched decision that scared you but turned out well? Does that memory compete with the doubt, or does the doubt override it?

**Q8:** What would it take for you to look back and say "that was the best decision I made that year"? Do you have any reason to believe that standard won't be met?

---

### 5. Schema-Driven Risk Inflation
*"Past trauma or learned behavior makes you over-weight certain risks, importing them into unrelated current situations."*

**Q9:** Was there a past financial mistake — yours or someone close to you — where a large purchase led to real harm? Is *this* situation the same, or structurally different?

**Q10:** What structural safeguards exist here that were absent in the past harm scenario? Buffer? Fallback? No debt? Cash payment? Your answer reveals whether the doubt is responsive to reality or to a ghost.
